Leadership

Through my time at USC, my experiences both inside and outside the classroom have taught me the importance of focusing on goals, communicating efficiently, and utilizing creativity for problem solving. All of these lessons have aided me in becoming not only a better student, but also a better researcher. Because of this, I have decided to pursue a Ph.D. in Computer Science at North Carolina State University. In my Junior year, I served as a mentor for a high school student studying Computer Science. She ended up confiding in me during our last meeting that the field was not for her for a variety of reasons, but mostly because she found it boring. It is unfortunate that many students drop out of STEM fields for similar reasons, even in college. Of course, it’s understandable if the field is not compatible with a student’s interests, however many don’t understand how expansive the field really is. For my first year of graduate school, I will be funded as a teaching assistant. This will give me the opportunity to use what I have learned at my time at USC to help students realize that Computer Science is not a dry field, but an expansive field that is applicable to many different interests.

In many introductory classes, there are plenty of group projects and presentations, but students often miss the purpose of them. As a teaching assistant, I hope to highlight the value of these projects to students. With the projects I will assign, this will help students to keep in mind the overarching goal of the assignment, or the problem that they must solve, and find a creative solution to the task at hand. I hope to incorporate a good amount of communication in these projects, either by enforcing communication within groups or as a presentation. These values are all extremely necessary for life after college. There is a misconception that students in STEM fields do not need communication skills or are not in a creative field, when that is definitely not the case. I will ensure that students understand the skills that are expected of a strong candidate in their field and teach them how to improve those skills through these projects. I will know if I have succeeded based on the grades they receive on their assignments and their evaluations of me in the classroom. I hope, by using these insights I have had at USC, that I can encourage students to view Computer Science as an exciting field and provide them with the necessary skills to succeed after college.
